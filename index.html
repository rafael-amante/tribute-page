Steve Irwin

Here's a time line of Steve Irwin's life:

  • 1962 – Steve Irwin is born in Essendon, Victoria, Australia. From the start, his life was shaped by nature—his parents were passionate about wildlife and soon moved to Queensland to start a reptile park.
  • 1970 – Beerwah Reptile Park opens, founded by his parents Bob and Lyn Irwin. This park would eventually become the world-famous Australia Zoo.
  • 1979 – Steve becomes a volunteer for Queensland’s East Coast Crocodile Management program. Over the years, he captures and relocates over 100 crocodiles, honing his skills and building his deep respect for the animals.
  • 1992 – Steve marries Terri Raines, an American naturalist. Their honeymoon becomes the filming trip for their first documentary—featuring Steve jumping into croc-infested waters!
  • 1998 – Steve and Terri welcome their first child, Bindi Sue Irwin, named after Steve's favorite croc and dog.
  • 2003 – Robert Clarence Irwin is born, Steve and Terri’s second child.
  • 2006 – Steve Irwin tragically passes away after a stingray injury while filming Ocean’s Deadliest. His death shocked the world, but also brought global attention to marine conservation.
